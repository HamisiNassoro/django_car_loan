Hereâ€™s a numbered list of the requirements:

  1. Joint Registration: The applicant (borrower) must undertake to have the car registered jointly in the name of State Officers and Civil Servants Motor Car Loan Scheme Fund under him/her.
  2. Comprehensive Insurance: The applicant must ensure that the vehicle is covered by full comprehensive insurance throughout the loan period; otherwise, the board will renew the insurance and debit the applicant's loan account.
  3. Cost of Valuation and Legal Fees: Civil servants are required to meet the cost of valuation and legal fees before receiving the loan.
  4. Driving License: Only civil servants with valid driving licenses are eligible for the loan.
  5. Monthly Installments: Civil servants must pay the amount due under the purchase agreement in monthly installments, which are deducted directly from their salaries.
  6. Certified Sale Agreement: Applicants must provide a certified copy of the sale agreement relating to the acquired car.
  7. Valuation Report: Civil servants must present a valuation report from an approved valuer.
  8. Known Vehicle Supplier: The vehicle's supplier must be known.
  9. First-Come, First-Served Basis: Applications are processed on a first-come, first-served basis after the duly completed application form is returned.
  10. Life Insurance: Life insurance is a prerequisite for applying for a car loan.
  11. Loan Continuation upon Termination: If the borrower ceases to be a civil servant, they must continue repaying the loan until completion.
